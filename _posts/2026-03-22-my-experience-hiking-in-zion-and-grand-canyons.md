I’ve been on several trips where I have walked a couple of miles with beautiful sceneries but spending a week with friends hiking the panoramic canyons in the southwest has been nothing short of an outstanding and once-in-a-lifetime experience.

I want to start off with what hiking has taught me. 

I am more unfit than the average person. I walked across rugged terrain, paths that looked like random displacements of rocks, dust and color. Stepping, and jumping over, pushing up and climbing with both hands, sliding and being cautious of not falling and tripping, all of this and keeping the status quo. We were 6 of us, and I was always left behind. There was a moment during the wretched 5 mile roundtrip hike towards Angel’s Landing, where I wanted to give up. We just kept walking up, climbing 100s of broken stairs, and gaining 100s of feet in mere minutes. I really felt weak at the moment, because all my friends were ahead of me, stronger than me, always stopping and waiting for me to catch my breath.

I always want to give my best to everything so it helps me not to give up. But knowing that I am the worst at something makes me quiver mentally - that makes me want to surrender. 

Sometimes in life we need to fail again and again. I felt like a failure during high school but got back up during my undergrad with specific goals. After spending a couple of months in Michigan, I feel like I have peaked failure. I have reached a point of humility where everyone seems to be doing better than me, and I need to restart from the ground up. Why I’m saying that it’s peaked is because I am going to start climbing again. I have found an area which sparks my interest and I will master it and keep working no matter what.

Make good friends. Just like the ones who would wait for me - don’t leave a hiker behind. I’m blessed to share this experience with them. And they continue to help me in my life everyday.
